Tsunami Safety Guidelines

Do's Don'ts
1. Know the Warning Signs - Understand the signs of a tsunami, such as strong and sudden sea-level changes. 1. Don't ignore tsunami warnings or alarms.
2. Have an Evacuation Plan - Know your evacuation route and designated meeting place. 2. Don't wait for official instructions if you're in a tsunami-prone area; evacuate immediately if you feel a strong earthquake near the coast.
3. Move to Higher Ground - Head inland or to higher ground as quickly as possible if a tsunami is imminent. 3. Don't stay near the beach or low-lying coastal areas.
4. Stay Informed - Listen to local authorities and emergency broadcasts for updates and instructions. 4. Don't rely solely on sirens; use multiple sources of information to confirm tsunami alerts.
5. Help Others - Assist children, the elderly, and those with disabilities in evacuating to safety. 5. Don't leave vulnerable individuals behind.
6. Be Cautious Afterward - Return to the coastal area only when authorities confirm it's safe to do so. 6. Don't assume the danger is over immediately after the initial tsunami; there may be multiple waves.
